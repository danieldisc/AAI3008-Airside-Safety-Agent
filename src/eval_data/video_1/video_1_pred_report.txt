### OVERALL INCIDENT SUMMARY
A crew member consistently violated safety protocols by standing dangerously close to an active aircraft propeller. This sustained breach of guidelines highlighted a significant disregard for safety exclusion zones.

### COMBINED NARRATIVE SEQUENCE
Initially, a crew member was observed standing close to a running aircraft propeller, repeatedly breaching the 7.5m exclusion zone for active engines. Over consecutive frames, the individual moved within a perilous proximity to the propeller, risking ingestion or impact. This behavior was persistent across both time intervals, with no apparent corrective action or acknowledgment of the imminent danger.

### RULE REFERENCE & VERDICT
The following safety rules from the Changi Airside Safety Directive (CASD) were violated:
1. **Exclusion Zones:** 7.5m buffer from active engine/propeller.
2. **Ingestion Hazard:** 5m radius suction zone.
  
**Final Status:** VIOLATION

### COMPREHENSIVE ROOT CAUSE OBSERVATION
The root cause appears to be a systemic disregard for established safety zones. The crew member demonstrated either a lack of awareness of the potential hazards or willful negligence. This could be attributed to insufficient safety training or failure in maintaining situational awareness amidst active aircraft operations.

### OVERALL INCIDENT SUMMARY
A person was observed repeatedly violating safety protocols by entering the exclusion zone near an active propeller. This dangerous behavior persisted across multiple flagged moments, indicating a significant disregard for established safety directives.

### COMBINED NARRATIVE SEQUENCE
At the beginning of the footage, a crew member is seen standing alarmingly close to an active propeller, within the danger zone of 7.5 meters. The individual proceeds to inspect or handle areas around the propeller without acknowledging the associated hazards. This engagement continues over several frames, with the crew member consistently remaining in proximity to the propeller, disregarding the safety buffer. Their actions suggest a focus on a task that seems to override typical safety considerations, possibly indicating either an urgent repair or routine inspection conducted unsafely.

### RULE REFERENCE & VERDICT
The following Changi Airside Safety Directives (CASD) were violated:
1. **Exclusion Zones**: Maintaining a 7.5 meter buffer from an active engine/propeller.
2. **Ingestion Hazard**: Proximity well within the 5 meter radius suction zone.
   
**Verdict**: VIOLATION

### COMPREHENSIVE ROOT CAUSE OBSERVATION
The persistent presence of the crew member within the active danger zone suggests a systemic disregard for the exclusion zones. This behavior might stem from inadequate training or safety culture, a misunderstanding of the seriousness of these zones, or a perceived urgency in their task that overshadowed the immediate risks. The incident points to the need for reinforced safety training and procedural compliance to prevent recurrence.

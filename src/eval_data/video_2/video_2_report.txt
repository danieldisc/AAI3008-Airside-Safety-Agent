OVERALL INCIDENT SUMMARY
Throughout the reviewed 10-second footage, Person A positioned themselves directly in front of an active commercial jet engine. Person A then attempted to retrieve a piece of luggage from a transport cart positioned directly within the ingestion zone of the running engine, demonstrating a severe and critical breach of safety, which is to stay 7.5m away from critical areas, in this case the engine intake area, of the aircraft.

COMBINED NARRATIVE SEQUENCE
Chunk 1 (00:00 - 00:10): Begins with the aircraft engine active, evidenced by the extreme suction forces visible at the intake. 
From 00:00, Person A is observed standing immediately in front of the engine cowling, well within the restricted safety buffer. 
At 00:02, Person A reaches to manipulate a piece of luggage on the adjacent cart. The aerodynamic suction of the active engine immediately ingests the luggage, pulling it violently into the turbine and striking Person A, causing them to fall to the tarmac. Person A remains on the ground in the critical danger zone while a secondary crew member attempts to signal an emergency shutdown.

RULE REFERENCE & VERDICT
VIOLATION

CASD Rule 1 (EXCLUSION ZONES): The individual breached the minimum Safety Buffer of 7.5 meters from an active aircraft engine.

CASD Rule 2 (INGESTION HAZARD): The individual entered and operated within the 5m front radius of a running engine, resulting in a catastrophic foreign object debris (FOD) ingestion event and physical impact from the suction forces.

COMPREHENSIVE ROOT CAUSE OBSERVATION
Based on the visual evidence, the root cause of this violation is a severe procedural failure regarding equipment staging and personnel positioning before engine spool-down. The luggage cart was parked directly inside the critical ingestion zone, and Person A attempted to perform ground handling duties while the engine was still producing dangerous suction forces, completely ignoring the lethal ingestion hazard.

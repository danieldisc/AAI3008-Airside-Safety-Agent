OVERALL INCIDENT SUMMARY
Throughout the reviewed 5-second footage, Person A (wearing a high-visibility vest) ran directly toward and into the intake of an active commercial jet engine. Person A was violently pulled into the engine, demonstrating a severe and critical breach of safety, which is to stay 7.5m away from critical areas, in this case the engine intake area, of the aircraft.

RULE REFERENCE & VERDICT
VIOLATION

CASD Rule 1 (EXCLUSION ZONES): The individual breached the minimum Safety Buffer of 7.5 meters from an active aircraft engine.

CASD Rule 2 (INGESTION/STRIKE HAZARD): The individual entered the 5m front radius of a running engine, resulting in a catastrophic ingestion event by the turbine blades.

COMPREHENSIVE ROOT CAUSE OBSERVATION
Based on the visual evidence, the root cause of this violation is an active threat on the tarmac. Person A was actively fleeing from an armed individual, which induced a state of panic and caused a complete loss of situational awareness regarding the lethal ingestion hazard posed by the active aircraft engine. The individual prioritized immediate evasion over airside safety protocols, running directly into the engine's aerodynamic suction zone.
